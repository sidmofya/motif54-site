The problem

The hardest decisions are made without the right people in the room.

High-consequence capital decisions in Africa rarely fail for lack of analysis. They fail because the people who hold the missing context — operators, regulators, co-investors, sponsors — are never in the same conversation at the same time.

Decision Rooms convene that conversation, in confidence, around a specific opportunity or question.

Outcomes, not events

What a room is for.

A Decision Room is judged by the decision it improves. It helps participants:

  • See what is actually happening beneath the headlines
  • Understand who controls the bottlenecks
  • Separate real risk from overstated or misunderstood risk
  • Agree what should happen first, second, and third
  • Decide whether to enter, wait, partner, invest, convene, or walk away

Bring a specific decision.

Decision Rooms work best when there is a live thesis, capital question, geography, sector, corridor, or partner landscape to align around.
